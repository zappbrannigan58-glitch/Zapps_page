Welcome to the Captains Log!

Today was my first day back to work after being off for a week. I found out when I went in that the AC was not working and had not been working since the middle of last week. So that's fun! But lets be positive, that's what I want to see in the world so that is what I need to put out in the world.

I currently work as a Site IT Analyst for a national company that operates across the US, Canada, and Puerto Rico. I have been working in Site IT for around 8 years, and in the past year or so I have been working as the active manager for a team of 3 people. My team is responsible for all 750 stores, all dealers, all remote sales people, 2 plants, 4 distribution centers, and 1 remote office. So while I don't have a lot of direct reports, it is a lot of responsibility.

Additionally my company has been going through a divestiture since 2025. We completed the divestiture, so I guess we are no longer going through it, but as a result things have been crazy busy and hectic.

Whats funny though is that now that the dievestiture work is complete, things are starting to slow down and get back to a more "normal" pace. Things were so crazy for so long though that now I feel an impending sense of doom because I'm like we are not busy anymore! People are going to get fired! Which is not the case, but it was so crazy and hard to manage all the things that were going on, that now since things are normal it feels like we are slow, which again is not the case. I guess my nerves have been fried and normal feels wrong.

I still feel bummed about the whole divestiture situation though because my original plan was to work for the parent company we were a part of until I retired since the company has been around for over 100 years and seemed like a solid investment for my time and resources. I know that people say to maximize your salary you should move around every 3 to 5 years and job hop, but I dunno. I like the job I do, and more importantly I like the majority of people I work with. I still do even though we are not the same company as before.

I have this fear though, and I know it's crazy, but I still have it. What if I'm not as good at my job as I think I am? What if the only reason I do it successfully is because of the relationships I've built over years at the same company? I can say with little modesty that I know I am pretty good at it. I may not be the best, but I'm certainly not the worst. But, what if I go somewhere else and I find out that the whole organization is pretty bad, and I only seem good in comparison to how bad we are? You know, like the big fish in a small pond kind of theory.

Fantasy Book Thoughts

I expressed in my first blog how I want to work on writing some books, particularly a fantasy, mystery, or sci-fi novel. I want to get something down "on paper" so to speak so I can start building toward that direction.

Fantasy World

I have a pretty well rounded and set up world in place for my fantasy universe. This stems from when I was in high school and my friends and I would play TTRPG games.

At the time a DnD book was $50 for a players handbook and the same for a monster manual, as well as a dungeon masters manual. That means to get started in the DnD universe I would need $150, which was a lot of money at the time (equivalent of $279.42 today). This was also when the internet was relatively new (early 2000's) and while napster, limewire, and other p2p services existed I didn't know what a PDF was at the time. It also never dawned on me try to find these materials online.

Due to the high cost of entry to play DnD I took my understanding of the game from the stater set I had ($20) and built my own world. I will base my fantasy book off that world and the background I created for the game when I was 14.

I named my game at the time Konvlite. A name I made up by rolling dice and taking the results and as a direct correlation with the alphabet. So I would roll a d20 and a d6 and if the result was 2 the word would start with a B. I then used some common sense to take the results and construct a name. 14 year old me thought it was pretty good. 40 year old me does not like it at all. My wife maintains it's good, but I still don't care for it
